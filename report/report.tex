\section{Motivation and question}

Standard reinforcement learning knows the dynamics $T$ of a Markov decision process and wants the optimal policy $\pi^{*}$. Many real settings invert that assumption. The paper this thesis builds on [1] uses an intensive care unit as its running example, where outcomes are observable but how a patient responds to a particular dose is not, and what we see is a batch of clinician decisions that are close to optimal but not exactly optimal.

My broader interest is in predictive wellness and longevity, the platform I am building under the working name \emph{Quantivida}. The consumer frontier today has two shapes: wearables (Apple Watch, Oura, WHOOP, continuous glucose monitors) collecting dense longitudinal physiology, and biomarker services like Function Health collecting rich but infrequent blood panels. Both feed downstream pipelines that fit population-level supervised models from \emph{user action} to \emph{outcome}, treating every body as a shared-dynamics black box and prescribing one-size-fits-all interventions. ITL takes a different posture: when a person makes near-optimal decisions about their own health, those decisions encode information about how their personal physiology responds, and that signal is exactly what maximum likelihood over counts throws away. Recovering individualized dynamics from those decisions is the methodological gap I want to work in. The semester's technical question: given $R$ and a batch of $\varepsilon$-optimal expert transitions $D = \{(s, a, s')\}$, can we recover $T^{*}$ in a way that respects the optimality information in the actions?

\section{Method and tools}

An action $a$ is $\varepsilon$-optimal at state $s$ when $\max_{a'} Q^{*}(s, a'; T) {-} Q^{*}(s, a; T) {\le} \varepsilon$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:mech}d). Observing the expert choose $a$ at $s$ yields an inequality on $Q^{*}$. Writing the policy-evaluation inverse $v^{\pi} = (I {-} \gamma P^{\pi})^{-1} r^{\pi}$ and substituting the Laplace-smoothed MLE $T^{\mathrm{MLE}}$ inside it linearizes the constraint in $T$, giving
\begin{equation*}
\min_{T} \;\; \tfrac{1}{2}\sum_{s,a,s'} N_{s,a,s'} \bigl(T_{s,a,s'} {-} T^{\mathrm{MLE}}_{s,a,s'}\bigr)^{2}
\quad \text{s.t.} \quad
\text{$\varepsilon$-ball on $(s,a) \in D$, simplex on $T$.}
\end{equation*}
The solver alternates between $\hat\pi$ (uniform over observed actions; uniform over all actions at unobserved states) and the QP, terminating when the $\varepsilon$-ball property holds at every observed state (Fig.~\ref{fig:mech}a). The convex pieces map onto Krause and H\"ubotter [2, Ch.\ 10, 11]: Bellman optimality, the linear policy-evaluation system, and the Dirichlet, Categorical conjugacy that makes the Laplace-smoothed MLE a posterior mean rather than a frequentist estimate. Implementation: CVXPY with an OSQP, SCS, CLARABEL fallback chain.

\section{Simulation: coverage sweep on Gridworld}

I reproduced the paper's central claim (their Figure~2) on a 5$\times$5 Gridworld with soft walls (penalty $-5$), slip $0.2$, $\gamma{=}0.95$, $\varepsilon{=}5.0$, 40\% stochastic expert. \emph{Coverage} is the fraction of states appearing in $D$; for each observed $(s,a)$ I sampled 10 transitions. At full coverage over the 50 seed sweep, ITL achieves Normalized Value $0.998 \pm 0.004$ vs.\ MLE's $0.141 \pm 0.004$, with $0.00$ vs.\ $7.24$ constraint violations, $\varepsilon$-matching $1.000$ vs.\ $0.710 \pm 0.017$, and best matching $0.870 \pm 0.070$ vs.\ $0.534 \pm 0.048$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:mech}c). MLE under-counts visited next-state cells under a stochastic expert, so its greedy policy walks into soft walls; ITL forces the expert's observed choices to remain $\varepsilon$-optimal under $\hat T$. Shape matches the paper's Figure~2; absolute scale runs $\sim 1.5\times$ larger than Table~4, almost certainly because the soft-wall layout is not published in detail. The structural pattern (ITL $\ge$ MLE on every metric, $\varepsilon$-matching $\to 1.0$ at full coverage per Theorem~1) reproduces on a 100-run RandomWorld sweep, although the gap is smaller there because RandomWorld's diffuse dynamics put MLE much closer to truth.

\section{Open question: joint reward and dynamics recovery}

The paper assumes $R$ is known. In MIMIC-IV that is fragile: the survival proxy reward ($\pm 15$ for discharge or mortality, after [4]) is hand-engineered. In the longevity setting it is even more fragile, since healthspan and recovery are noisy proxies inferred from the very signals we are modeling. The extension lifts $R$ into a decision variable $R(s,a) = \Phi(s,a)^{\top} w$ and solves for $(T, w)$ jointly (Fig.~\ref{fig:mech}b). The $\varepsilon$-ball constraint becomes
\begin{equation*}
\bigl(\Phi(s,a) {-} \Phi(s,a')\bigr)^{\top} w {+} \gamma\,(T_{s,a} {-} T_{s,a'})^{\top} v_{\mathrm{lin}} \;\ge\; \varepsilon,
\end{equation*}
bilinear in $(T, w)$ but a convex QP at each step of the alternation. An L1 penalty on $w$ plus an anchor at one $(s,a)$ pin the additive ambiguity. The prototype converges in two outer iterations on both the 3 state corridor and the 5$\times$5 Gridworld; with one-hot $(s,a)$ features ($d{=}100$), goal anchored, deterministic expert, ITL+IRL recovers $\pi^{*}$ exactly (best action $1.000$ vs.\ ITL $1.000$ vs.\ MLE $0.600$; $T$ MSE $0.4873$ vs.\ $0.4929$ vs.\ $0.5154$). \emph{Honest caveat:} this gridworld is goal dominated. Pairing $T_{\mathrm{MLE}}$ with a trivial reward $R{=}10\cdot\mathbf{1}[s{=}s^{*}]$ recovers $\pi^{*}$ at the same rate as $R^{*}$, so a $1.000$ match is necessary but not sufficient evidence that the IRL step is doing real work. A two-goal benchmark and a head-to-head against MaxEnt-IRL [3] (wired in via L-BFGS-B) are the next falsifiers.

\paragraph{Implications and roadmap.} For predictive longevity this means modeling how a specific person's biomarker trajectory evolves under their own choices, rather than fitting a population-average dose response. Recovery scores and healthspan signals are noisy user proxies, so jointly inferring $R$ with $T$ removes the most fragile assumption in any wellness pipeline. The continuous-state lift is the gating step before this transfers to raw sensor streams and is the thread Quantivida is built around.
